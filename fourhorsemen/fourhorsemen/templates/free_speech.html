Free Speech vs. Hate Speech

We asked our participants whether, when designing an algorithm to prevent harassment, it would be more important to prevent:

  • Non-harassing content being flagged as harassing and potentially subjected to remediation
  • Harassing content not being flagged as harassing and not subjected to remediation
  • No preference

We were surprised by the finding that three times as many people wanted to prevent false negatives, that is, having genuinely harassing content get through the filters and reach the public or its target. This is a big surprise because there is a vocal minority that advocates strongly that this type of content should be considered “free speech.”
